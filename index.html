Game Overview

BASED-FLAPPY is a Web3 competitive gaming experience built on the Base blockchain. Unlike traditional games where each round is standalone, this contest rewards consistency and dedication. Every game you play adds to your TOTAL SCORE for the week. The player with the highest cumulative score at the end of the week wins a share of the ETH prize pool. This unique mechanic ensures that skilled players who put in more time have a significantly higher chance of winning compared to someone who gets lucky once.

How It Works

The weekly contest runs from Monday 00:00 UTC to Sunday 23:59:59 UTC. To participate, you need a MetaMask wallet connected. After paying the 0.00005 ETH entry fee, each game you play counts toward your total. Your TOTAL SCORE is the sum of all individual game scores achieved during that week. The prize pool grows as more players enter, making it potentially more rewarding as participation increases. The top two players by total score split the prize pool (50% to 1st, 40% to 2nd, 10% protocol fee).

Gameplay Mechanics

Tap the screen or press SPACE to make the bird fly upward. The bird responds with smooth, physics-based movement with a gentle float. Pipes appear from the right side with random gap positions. Navigate through gaps to score points. Each pipe passed equals one point. The game ends when you hit a pipe, the ceiling, or the ground. The slow, controlled physics make it easy to learn but challenging to master.

Scoring System

Only scores from entered games are counted. Without paying the entry fee, your scores are not saved. You can play multiple times during the week to increase your total. The leaderboard shows cumulative scores, so playing more games gives you a strategic advantage over someone who plays fewer times. This encourages active participation and rewards dedication over luck.

Prize Distribution

The prize pool is the total of all entry fees collected during the week. It is distributed as follows: 50% goes to the player with the highest total score, 40% to the second-highest, and 10% goes to the protocol as a maintenance fee. If only one player enters, they still get 90% of the pool if they achieve any score.
